Testing for cations:

Flame Tests

Method: Heat a small amount of each salt sample on an inert NiChrome wire (cleaned using conc. HCl) in a blue Bunsen flame.

Flame

Colour

Cation

Also note Flame Photometry (F/AES) which uses a calibration curve of standard solutions to identify the metal ion and its concentration from the light it emits.

Testing for anions:

Halides test

Method: Add a few drops of dilute nitric acid to a few drops of salt solution, followed by a few drops of silver nitrate solution.

Colour

Halide

Sulfate ions test

Method: Add a few drops of dilute nitric acid to a few drops of salt solution, followed by a few drops of barium chloride solution.

If sulfate ions are present, a white precipitate should form.

Carbonate ions test

Method: Add a few drops of dilute nitric acid to a few drops of salt solution

If carbonate ions are present, bubbles should be given off.
